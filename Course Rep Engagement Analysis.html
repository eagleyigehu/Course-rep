Why is engagement falling?

Primary: loss of meaning (most impactful)
Comment 1
"I wanted to be a Rep but I wasn't really sure what was expected of me after the initial training."
Comment 4
"I submitted feedback but never heard what happened afterwards."
Comment 5
"The role feels important but sometimes it feels disconnected from my day-to-day student experience."
Comment 8
"I wasn't sure about raising issues with the EQ team when I could just go to my course staff directly."
Secondary: practical barriers
Comment 2
"Most communication comes through email and I honestly miss a lot of it."
Comment 7
"There are so many emails from different parts of the university that Arts SU messages can get buried."
Comment 3
"My course timetable changes frequently and it's difficult to attend extra meetings."
Comment 10
"When changes happen because of student feedback it's really motivating, but I don't always have the time because I have to work to afford my studies and transport to college."
Comment 9
"I attended training at the start of the year but forgot a lot of the information later on."

Three recommendations

Prioritised by impact and ease of implementation.

Recommendation 1 / highest priority
Close the feedback loop
Every time student feedback leads to a real change, explicitly tell reps: your feedback caused this. When reps can see the outcome of their work, they re-engage. This directly addresses Comments 4 and 10.
Lowest cost Highest impact
Recommendation 2
Move communication to instant channels
Shift from email to WhatsApp or Instagram, with a brief monthly summary. Email is getting buried (Comments 2, 7). Reps are students; they live on their phones, not in their inboxes.
Medium effort Addresses comms barrier
Recommendation 3
Offer async participation options
Record all meetings and share concise summaries. Reps with timetable conflicts or work commitments (Comments 3, 10) can stay informed without attending live.
Requires setup Addresses time barrier
